Common Phishing Red Flags

Suspicious URLs

Always hover over links before clicking. Phishing URLs often contain misspellings (e.g., paypa1.com), extra subdomains, or strange TLDs like .tk, .xyz.

Urgency & Fear Tactics

Phrases like "Act immediately!", "Your account will be closed in 24 hours", or "Unusual activity detected" are designed to make you panic and click without thinking.

Credential Requests

Legitimate companies never ask for your password, SSN, credit card number, or bank account details via email.

Sender Mismatch

Check the actual sender address — not just the display name. PayPal <support@paypal-secure-verify.ml> is NOT from PayPal.

Too-Good-To-Be-True Offers

You haven't won an iPhone 15, a cash prize, or a free vacation. If it sounds too good to be true, it almost certainly is a scam.

Unexpected Attachments

Never open unexpected attachments — especially .exe, .zip, .docm, or .pdf files from unknown senders.

What To Do Instead

Report It

Forward suspicious emails to your IT/security team. Most email clients have a "Report Phishing" button.

Delete It

Do not reply, do not click links, do not open attachments. Delete the email immediately.

Verify Independently

If an email claims to be from your bank or a service you use, go directly to their official website (type the URL yourself) or call them.

Use MFA

Enable multi-factor authentication on all accounts. Even if your password is compromised, MFA adds an extra layer of protection.
